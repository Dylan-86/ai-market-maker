Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about trading on Dexetera

Dexetera allows you to trade any verifiable, measurable market. This includes traditional assets like cryptocurrencies, commodities, and stocks, as well as custom futures contracts you can create yourself. If there's a reliable data source for it, you can trade it on Dexetera.

Dexetera leverages Arbitrum's high-speed infrastructure to offer gas-free trading. We've optimized our smart contracts and use meta-transactions to cover gas costs, allowing you to execute trades with just one click, without worrying about gas fees.

Yes, Dexetera is fully decentralized. We don't have access to your funds, there are no middlemen, and no company takes fees from your trades. The platform operates with a fully on-chain order book, and all trades are settled directly on the Arbitrum blockchain.

Creating a custom market on Dexetera is simple. You'll need to define the underlying asset or metric you want to trade, set the contract parameters (including the 1-year expiration), and specify the data source that will be used for settlement. Our interface guides you through the process step-by-step.

Dexetera has no platform fees. The only costs you'll encounter are the standard blockchain network fees (which we cover for you on Arbitrum) and the spread between bid and ask prices. There are no fees paid to any company - all value stays within the trading ecosystem.

All trades on Dexetera are settled on-chain through smart contracts. When a futures contract expires, it automatically settles based on the verifiable data source you specified when creating the market. Settlement is transparent, automatic, and recorded on the Arbitrum blockchain for anyone to verify.
